A Tribute to Stephen Hawking

"I have noticed even people who claim everything is predestined, and that we can do nothing to change it, look before they cross the road." - Stephen Hawking

Hawking at NASA - 1980

Hawking was a remarkable theoretical physicist. Despite having ALS, Hawking fought against all odds and lived a very long life. This page is dedicated to his life long passion for physics and all he has done for the scientific community. Below are some of his major accomplishments:

  • 1971: Awarded the Gravity Research Foundation Award
  • 1973: Published his first book, "The Large Scale Structure of Space-Time"
  • 1974: Discovered Hawking radiation
  • 1975: Awarded the Eddington Medal and the Pius XI Gold Medal
  • 1976: Awarded the Dannie Heineman Prize, the Maxwell Prize and the Hughes Medal
  • 1978: Awarded the Albert Einstein Award
  • 1981: Proposed that information in black holes are lost when they evaporate
  • 1983: Hartle & Hawking published the Hartleâ€“Hawking state model.
  • 1988: Published his book, "A Brief History of Time"
  • 2015: Proposed that information is not neccesarily lost when it enters a black hole and that it might be possible to retrieve information from a black hole
  • 2018: Published his last paper, "A smooth exit from eternal inflation?"

Hawking passed away on March 14 2018. He was 76 years old.
